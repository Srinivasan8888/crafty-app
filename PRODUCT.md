## Users

Crafty serves two sides of India's handmade-craft ecosystem, plus the operators who curate it.

**Buyers / discoverers** (demand side, roughly 95% mobile). Example: Priya, 28, marketing manager in Bengaluru. She scrolls Instagram for handmade gift ideas, lurks in WhatsApp craft groups, occasionally attends Cubbon Park meetups. Her job: find a crochet artist, a yarn shop, or a Sunday workshop near her, and save the ones she likes. She arrives impulsive and low-commitment, on a phone, often on patchy 4G.

**Creators** (supply side, browse on phone but create on laptop):
- Hobbyist crafters turned sellers (Aisha, 32, sells crochet on weekends) who want discovery beyond their Instagram circle.
- Supply-store owners (Mr. Krishnan, 55, Commercial Street yarn shop): tech-intimidated, English-second-language, just want to be findable.
- Studio / academy operators (Anjali, 40, Whitefield pottery studio) who want a steady stream of students.
- Event organizers / community leads (Rahul, runs the Cubbon meetups) promoting workshops beyond a WhatsApp group.

**Admins** (internal). The founder and trusted delegates who curate listings, pre-populate stores from public data, and moderate flags, working web-first on a laptop.

## Product Purpose

Crafty is India's city-localized discovery hub for the handmade-craft ecosystem: crafters, supply stores, learning studios, and craft events, found by city, starting in Bengaluru. Discovery today is fragmented across Instagram, WhatsApp groups, and word-of-mouth, and no one owns local craft discovery (Etsy is global and transactional, Instagram is content not discovery, Justdial is generic). Crafty gives crafters visibility, stores reach, studios students, and buyers one trusted place to find them.

Success is a living, indexable directory buyers return to: weekly active discovery sessions (users who view three or more listing pages a week), creators who self-onboard and stay, and city-by-city coverage that deepens over time.

## Brand Personality

**Warm, community-first, India-localized.** Three words: neighbourly, handmade, unhurried.

The voice is a friend who runs the local craft WhatsApp group, not a SaaS sales page. It speaks plainly ("Your shop name", not "Brand identity"), celebrates the maker over the metric, and treats listing as a five-minute favour rather than a funnel. Emotionally it should evoke the calm pride of a Sunday morning bazaar: discovery as a pleasure, belonging rather than performance.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI-SaaS marketing.** No gradient-text heroes, no big-number metric grids, no glassmorphism, no neon-on-dark "tool" aesthetic. If it could be any startup, it has failed.
- **Etsy and global marketplaces.** Crafty is local-first and discovery-first, not a global transactional storefront. No checkout-centric, conversion-optimized pressure.
- **Instagram.** Content feeds are not discovery. Crafty is structured, findable, city-scoped, not an infinite algorithmic scroll.
- **Justdial and generic directories.** Not a grey listings database. Editorial warmth and real craft photography, not stock icons and dense rows.
- **Leaderboard and popularity dynamics.** Heart counts stay private. No public vanity metrics, no brigading surface.

## Design Principles

1. **Local before global.** Every surface is scoped to a city. Relevance comes from "near me", not "trending everywhere". Never show a global feed.
2. **Discovery, not transaction.** Optimize for the joy of finding and saving, not for conversion. Contact happens off-platform; the product's job is the introduction, warmly made.
3. **Concierge warmth.** Behave like a trusted community host: inclusive copy, reassurance at anxious moments (first listing, first save), celebration at wins. Lower the barrier, then applaud.
4. **Mobile thumb, web craft.** Buyers discover with a thumb on a phone on 4G; creators build with care on a laptop. Each surface is designed for its real posture, never one shrunk to fit the other.
5. **Show the maker, not the metric.** Real faces, hands, and finished work carry the page. No vanity numbers, no popularity theatre. The human is the hero.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**: semantic HTML, visible focus, labelled forms, alt text required on uploads, skip-to-content.
- Touch targets at least 44px (buyers are thumb-first on phones).
- Honour `prefers-reduced-motion`, `prefers-color-scheme` (light and dark both first-class), and forced-colors mode.
- Indian-network reality: pages must render and stay usable on slow 4G and with a screen reader on a slow connection. Progressive enhancement, not JS-only.
- English-first today, with copy written plainly for English-second-language users, and i18n groundwork already in place.
